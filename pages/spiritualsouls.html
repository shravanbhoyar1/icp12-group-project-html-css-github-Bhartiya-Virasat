Gautama Buddha

Gautama Buddha, born in Lumbini around 563 BCE, was a prince who renounced worldly life in search of truth and attained enlightenment under the Bodhi tree. He founded Buddhism, teaching the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path as a way to overcome suffering and achieve Nirvana through mindfulness, compassion, and detachment.

Swami Vivekananda

Swami Vivekananda, born in 1863 in Kolkata, was a disciple of Sri Ramakrishna and a key figure in bringing Indian philosophy to the West. He taught the divinity of the soul, service to humanity, and the unity of all religions, famously representing India at the 1893 Parliament of World Religions in Chicago.

Guru Nanak

Guru Nanak Dev Ji, born in 1469 in Punjab, was the founder of Sikhism and a champion of spiritual equality. He preached devotion to one universal God (Ik Onkar), honest living, selfless service, and unity among all faiths, emphasizing love, humility, and the remembrance of God's name.
